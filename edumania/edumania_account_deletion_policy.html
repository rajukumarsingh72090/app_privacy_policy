Account Deletion Policy

Effective Date: March 24, 2026

At Edumania, we respect your right to privacy and data control. This Account Deletion Policy explains how you can request the deletion of your account and the personal data associated with it.

How to Delete Your Account

You have the right to delete your Edumania account at any time. We provide two convenient methods for you to do so:

Method 1: In-App Deletion (Recommended)

The fastest and most direct way to delete your account is through the Edumania application itself. Follow these steps:

  1. Open the Edumania app on your device.
  2. Navigate to your Profile or Account Settings.
  3. Scroll down to find the Delete Account option.
  4. Tap on it and follow the on-screen prompts to confirm your decision. You may be asked to re-authenticate for security purposes.

Method 2: Email Request

If you are unable to access the app or prefer to request deletion via email, you can do so by contacting our support team.

  1. Send an email to rajukumarsingh72090@gmail.com from the email address associated with your Edumania account.
  2. Use the subject line: "Account Deletion Request".
  3. In the body of the email, state your request to delete your account and associated data.

Note: For email requests, we may need to verify your identity before proceeding with the deletion to protect your security.

What Happens When Your Account is Deleted

Upon receiving and validating your deletion request (or immediately if done via the app), the following actions are taken:

  • Personal Data: Your profile information (name, email, profile picture) is permanently removed from our active databases and Firebase Authentication systems.
  • App Activity: Your course progress, saved items, and personalized learning data are deleted.
  • Loss of Access: You will immediately lose access to the account and any active subscriptions or purchased content tied to it. This action cannot be undone.

Data Retention Exceptions

While we strive to delete all your personal data, we may retain certain limited information for legal, security, or operational reasons:

  • Transactional Data: Information regarding past purchases or financial transactions may be securely retained as required by tax and accounting laws.
  • Security Logs: Anonymized server logs may be temporarily kept for security and fraud prevention purposes before being routinely purged.
